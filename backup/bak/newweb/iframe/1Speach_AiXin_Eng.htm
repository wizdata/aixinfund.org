Let’s Join Hands in the Fight Against AIDS

By Yongkai Weng, President of The AiXin Foundation

 

Dear Friends, Ladies and Gentlemen,

On behalf of Aixin Foundation, it is my honor to have you here today in celebration of the publication of our new book, “AIDS in China.”

As a grave social and public health problem today, AIDS is a universal challenge confronting all societies regardless of geographical location, historical background, economic development, or political culture. And China is not an exception.  Unfortunately, the battle against AIDS in China has silently and rapidly reached a critical point.  “For the truth is, today, China stands on the brink of an explosive AIDS epidemic,” the UN Secretary General Kofi Annan warned in 2002, “There is no time to lose if China is to prevent a massive further spread of HIV/AIDS.  China is facing a decisive moment.”  The number of people infected with HIV/AIDS in China could rise to 10 million by the year 2010 unless both the government and the public act urgently and effectively to prevent such an epidemic.  China is the largest country in Asia, and the most populated country in the world. Since the 1980s, China has launched one of the most rapid and wide-range social-economic reforms in the world. Entering the 21st Century, China is reaching a new stage of prosperity, with an unprecedented pace of economic development. Together with the trend for globalization, and the interdependence between China and all other countries, the future of China, and the well being of the Chinese people, will have a significant impact on the rest of the world.  Therefore, the prevention and control of HIV/AIDS in China is relevant to us all.

 

In the last few years, the Chinese government, non-government organizations, healthcare professionals, grassroots activists, and international AIDS communities have made many outstanding achievements.  The Chinese government has been highly committed to HIV/AIDS prevention and control, especially since the end of the 2002.  Numerous efforts have been made, and positive results have been achieved.  However, the battle against AIDS is just beginning. HIV/AIDS prevention and control faces even greater challenges and remains a difficult task for China.  In recognition of the unwavering efforts of all those mentioned before, AiXin Foundation in the US and the Institute of Social Development Research in China have joined together to publish this book C AIDS in China C and to make it available to those concerned in China, the U.S., and beyond.

Up to this point, there has never been a book at the international level that offers a comprehensive discussion on the AIDS issue in China.  This book is intended to fill this gap:  to objectively present the complete experience of the fight against AIDS in China―history, current situation, roadblocks ahead, feasible solutions, the on-going efforts.  We hope that it will provide a better understanding of the AIDS situation in China.  This book includes a collection of informative writings with sound knowledge of various aspects of AIDS and related government policies in China.  The highlight topics included: the unique route of transmission, its impact on the society and economy, and government response and policies.

AiXin Foundation will freely distribute copies of this book to interested organizations, universities and public libraries in China, to be used for training of government officials, and for public health and AIDS education.  AiXin Foundation will also donate this book to the U. S. Library of Congress, the United Nations and other related international organizations, research institutions, and philanthropic foundations concerned with AIDS worldwide.  It is our hope that, through the dissemination of information, we can promote the empathy and compassion within the international AIDS community, and active participation of the concerned organizations.  Furthermore, we hope to elicit various forms of assistance and support for the Chinese people, and to forge a common bond to effectively deal with AIDS.

AiXin is a non-profit, non-political, publicly supported charitable organization founded in 2002 by a group of overseas Chinese in the greater Washington area.  All AiXin members are volunteers.  Currently, our top priority is to promote AIDS awareness and prevention in China.  We wish to build a bridge between China and international communities during the course of conquering AIDS.   AiXin’s work may be small in comparison to the tremendous efforts by Chinese and international organizations, but we all share a common vision and devotion to the same goal.  We hope that people in China and around the world will pay close attention to the AIDS problem in this huge populated country, and join hands in fighting the AIDS epidemic.

AiXin Foundation would like to pay our sincere tribute to our collaborator, the Institute of Social Development Research, especially to the chief editor, Professor Jin Wei, for her great contribution to this book.  We are deeply grateful to our partner, the International Publishing House for China’s Culture and its president, Mr. Zhao Xiaoming; and the Library of Congress, Asian Division, and its Chief, Dr. Hwa-Wei Lee.  Their generous support and effort make the publishing of this book and this event possible.  Our special thanks to Dr. Nicholas Eberstadt at the American Enterprise Institute, Dr. Bates Gill at the Center for Strategic and International Studies, and Journal of the Foreign Affairs. With their generous support and authorization, we are able to present you with their articles offering insightful analysis.  We also wish to extend our sincere appreciation to all editors and authors who participated in this book.  Their generous support and voluntary contribution has made the book possible. 

Finally, we wish to thank all the caring ordinary citizens at the frontline of the war against AIDS in China, we solute to them all.

Thank you so much for coming and we are looking forward to your continued support!
