Trip to Guizhou by Monica Yu
In October 2003, I took a trip to the remote villages in the mountainous region in GuiZhou province, China. This trip was led by a Zigen member (http://www.zigen.org/). Her main purpose was to monitor various Zigen projects, which included the training of the local women for a newly opened MaoPing Village Woman Center.
The villages and its surroundings were beautiful. They easily took your breath away. The locals, almost all Miao minority, were friendly, hardworking and filled with hospitalities. As an AiXin board member (as well as a Zigen supporter), I was also interested in collecting first-hand information on healthcare and health education in these rural areas. My stay was about 2 weeks. We only visited 10 villages and 12 schools, all located within LeiShan and TaiJiang Counties.
In a nutshell, I found out that there is no permanent physician or physician assistant available in any of the villages. I was told that other counties were the same. The following summarizes their current situation:
  • Access to medical care is unaffordable and inaccessible. The cost of healthcare can easily bankrupt a family's life saving;

  • Up to 40% of young people (mainly men) go to the city to find temporary jobs. There is a constant and desperate need to generate cash to support their families.

  • Most women do not have much knowledge about infectious diseases such as hepatitis, HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, etc. However, most of them heard about SARS.
This finding is worrisome, considering that the number of young people working in the city is on the rise. The chances of bringing infectious diseases back to the villages are high. The urgent need of health education and clinics in these rural areas is greater than ever!
