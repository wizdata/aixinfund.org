Journal

7/31/2010

      I was really excited to start this trip off. When I got to the hotel, I was excited to meet everyone and get this trip underway. The bus ride up was fun, and I got to meet everyone and get to know them better. Playing games with everyone let me get to know everyone better. It seemed that everyone already knew each other, but everyone was quite open. It’ll be fun to hang out with everyone and get to know them better.

     

I was mentally prepared for what we were going to see so I am ok with the sleeping and eating conditions. After spending so much time with my grandpa who lives in the countryside, this isn’t so bad, but it still is worse than I’ve ever experienced. After baking in the sun, it would be nice to have an electric fan or something at night because it is extremely hot. I do like taking cold water baths though; they’re a good relief to the hot day. My only concern is the bathroom and the sanitary stuff, but I don’t think that will really be that big of a problem. 

 

I really want to do well on this trip and make it good for the kids. Hopefully they’ll learn something here or at least enjoy the classes. I know what it feels like to have a mentor. We did this activity in elementary school where a middle school student would come down and help us and play with us for the entire day. I also sometimes help my little brother with his homework, and I know he enjoys it a lot. So I imagine YBB would be the same thing, just for an extended change and also more intense. I really want to make this special for the kids. I know this will be slightly tough because we do have a language barrier between us and the kids, but with the help of the college students, I don’t think this will be that big of a problem. I’m really looking forward to this.

 

8/01/2010

      Another grueling day of heat has passed without much incident. I am really sick of this heat already; I think it’s mainly that it’s humid and stuffy. At least it’s somewhat cool at night so we don’t have to suffer too much! The cafeteria food we had today wasn’t too bad, so I don’t really mind. As long as I’m not hungry then I’m fine.

 

      This trip will be an eye opening break from our normal lives in the US. The rural areas and the completely different living conditions will broaden my view of the world which I think is very beneficial in life. Often, I hear my friends talking about how they can’t get some certain item or aren’t allowed to do a certain activity, but to think they even have this opportunity should be good enough for them. I mean, I think those thoughts too and often am upset with my parents because they won’t allow me to do something or get me something. However, with my limited experience of rural areas in China, our lives in the US are very special, even if we are slightly below average. We live such privileged lives that we take even the little things for granted. We enjoy luxuries that we don’t even think are luxuries. That’s why I think this type of activity is very beneficial for teenagers and college students; it broadens our perspective of the world. I volunteered at a nursing home once and I talked with them, asking them if they had any advice about life. Enjoy the little things. Our lives are so filled with luxuries that we take for granted, if one day, everything that we take for granted disappeared, we would most likely fail as a society. When I’m much older and with a stable job and family, I think I would like to participate in programs like this, or help fund programs or give money to areas around the world.

 

Today, we mainly went over our plans and lessons for the next two weeks. I am amazed by how much work and effort EVERYONE has put into their lessons. In the lesson plans, almost everything is described right down to the final detail. I particularly like the Science and English class lessons. Doing experiments is a good way to keep the children involved and participating in the activity. I remember back in school all the boring science lectures that, for me, were nearly impossible to pay attention too. But, whenever we did experiments, science class became one of the best classes of the day. Also, the 4 experiments that we have planned are really cool and will be fun to do. The English Lesson Plan is also good. I don’t think it’s possible for us to teach them a lot of grammar or vocabulary in the 2 weeks that we have, but allowing them to be able to communicate comfortable in English will be amazing too! I am so looking forward to teaching this material to the kids.

 

8/02/2010

      It’s not that bad here, well sometimes. It was really cool in the morning and hearing all the animal calls and the natural sounds is a nice break from the ruckus of my life. I was actually really excited for today to start teaching. There’s something special about volunteering that makes it appealing to me. I remember after spending 5 hours volunteering at a nursing home; I was dead tired but I still felt a sense of fulfillment. By volunteering, I was able to help improve someone’s life. But I think that volunteering doesn’t just benefit the receivers, it benefits both parties. It’s a growing experience for the volunteers and a new, exciting experience for the receivers.

 

      I was nervous when entering my classroom. I was worried that they wouldn’t listen to us or that we wouldn’t be able to explain everything with our language barrier. But the students were all extremely polite and respectful. They respectfully listened and stopped talking when we started to speak. Personally, they acted much more respectful than the students at my school. Some of my classmates will continue to talk even while the teacher is talking. I’m thankful that the students here don’t do that. I can’t imagine 4-5 students in our class talking on and on while I was trying to speak.

 

Another thing that made the kids stand out was that they were all extremely eager to learn. I noticed that 4-5 students in our class stood out during our English lesson. They spoke well and articulated well. To me, it seemed that the students enjoyed picking out their English names, and I’m glad they had fun with it. Learning a foreign language is extremely difficult. But they embraced the idea and welcomingly participated in all the activities. We tried to keep everyone involved so we incorporated a lot of different games into the class. When we went around listening to them speak, none of them refused to participate. They were a little shy, but I was glad to see them step out of their comfort zone. I’m pretty sure I wasn’t that brave when I was their age. If a foreign student came up to me and asked me to speak, I would not utter a single word. Their courage and openness surprises me. I can’t stress this point enough.

 

Computer class was quite interesting. I’m glad most of the kids followed directions and didn’t play games on the computer, but some still did. I can imagine this is just like what my high school teachers feel; it’s frustrating. However, it seems that everyone is already familiar with the majority of the different programs except paint. So today, we mainly played around with paint. The kids really enjoyed drawing pictures and fooling around with paint. Some of them drew really nice pictures. The end project will for sure be a success with the skills these kids have working with computers.

 

This lecture was a real eye-opener. She spoke about kids living in horrendous conditions, often homeless and orphaned searching for food. It’s amazing how happy they seem in all the pictures though. These pictures really depict the common saying “Enjoy the little things.” Without any possessions in the world, these kids are happy and enjoy their lives. People like her are needed throughout the world; people who acknowledge the problems in the world and strive to solve the problems. The stories she told us seem almost too bad to be real, but I know they are and these kids aren’t the only ones. Millions of kids around the world are left starving, homeless, orphaned etc. I liked the way she showed pictures of the transitions of the kids. From the moment they arrived at the school, to present day. It really shows how a little kindness can grow and completely change the lives of a person. These kids went from lacking basic manners and etiquette, to being extremely polite. What also surprised me were the art pieces that the students made. The artwork really does show the patience and dedication these students have to what they’re doing. In America, this determination and dedication would be lost long before even half of the artwork was completed. I can never imagine what these kids went through. It just shows how privileged we really are.

 

8/03/2010

      After having one day full of class, I think today went much more smoothly. However, I don’t think that the English curriculum is difficult enough for them. All of the students already know the material and are quite good at it. So I think that for the next few days we might not follow the lesson plan and hopefully make it much more intriguing or make it slightly more difficult than before. I remember we asked them which class they thought was the most fun and most wanted to learn, and it turned out to be English. With a more difficult curriculum, they might be more interesting in learning. It seems to be just reviewing right now. I’m really enjoying teaching these kids. It gives me a chance to lead a group and improve someone’s life. A majority of the kids are very polite and listen to us, but there are some who still talk and don’t listen to what we are saying. But, personally, that is their loss and not ours. If they choose not to listen to us then they miss out on a chance for them to better themselves.

     

      I think music was a pretty good success today. At first, we tried playing them the PowerPoint, but class 4 had speakers and they drowned out our music. So we combined the two classes and had music together. I actually really liked the PowerPoint because it showed a variety of differing musical genres. For each song, we asked the class if they liked the music. A majority of the class liked music that had a good beat, but naturally, their favorite songs were Chinese because they can understand the lyrics. I think in the future, we should carry on with the class but at the end combine classes and have like a dance party of some sort. Energize the students and let them have fun. The only complaint the students had about the class was that it was too hot. So if we’re only in there for a short time it shouldn’t be too bad.

 

      Also, Travel Around the World was a pretty good success. I especially liked the PowerPoint the planning group created. We had the class guess each of the numbers for the 100 people activity. What really is surprising is the number of people who have a college degree. In 100 people, only 1 person has a college degree. Our class has approximately 50 people. So we wouldn’t have anyone with a college degree. However, the YBB program will surely help. In many countries, I think the problem is that the children lose interest in school; I realize that some students are forced to work, but we asked our class how many thought they were going to enter college, and only a few raised their hands. WE told them the importance of having a college degree and how important a good education is. This program isn’t about us; it’s about helping the children. Helping them realize the importance of education, bettering their lives.

 

      Social Studies was a great success. We turned the pre-test into a game for the students. Since we have 4 columns of desks in each room, we assigned each column a letter and read them the question and answers. We helped them translate each of the questions. We allowed the students to pick a column and answer the questions. Those who got it wrong could not play anymore, but the people who got it right were allowed to continue playing. Eventually, we asked all the questions and had to come up with 4 more, but the kids really enjoyed it. They didn’t want to take a break. So think this was a good start to introduce them to American History. I know how boring history class can be. When it’s boring, the students will immediately lose interest. We’ll strive to make each social studies more interesting and exciting.

I think the experiment was a good success. It was exceptionally hot so we decided that letting them play with some water outside would be a good for them to cool down and have fun. So, we did the experiment and explained why the cardboard didn’t fall and drew pictures. After explaining the procedure, we let the kids go out and try it. Each student tried the experiment, and then we basically just played with water. The girls were really shy, but when the boys threw water on them, the girls threw water back. It was fun and exciting, and everyone enjoyed it.

      I really like the college student’s project idea. The idea of Emergency Actions is great! Especially since this area is on a fault line, these lessons will be important for the students. Our college students are mainly focusing on fire and earthquake safety and what to do in those situations. Also, I think the college students are preparing an emergency care kit for our kids that we’re going to give them at the end of these 2 weeks.

 

8/05/2010

      We reviewed “I’m a Little Teapot” again today in class. Maybe once they learn all the lyrics, the students can perform the song and the dance at the end of the 2 weeks! I think it’d be really cute for our class to do so. After singing, we had the students complete their drawings from yesterday. Some of the drawings are exceptionally good. Instead of following the curriculum today, I decided that we should teach them what to say at a restaurant which is pretty simple. Tomorrow, I plan on having the students work on a skit for 10 minutes and present the skit. After that, I want the students to add a third member to their skit and have a conversation at the table while their food is being prepared. This will force the students to actually practice the language which I think is one of the best ways of learning a foreign language. When you are just drilled with vocabulary, most of the time it doesn’t stick because you don’t practice it. If we can let them practice the vocabulary that they’re taught, their English will improve. Also, by allowing them to practice speaking increases their interest in the language.

 

      Computer class didn’t exactly work out as planned because the power grid was going haywire. So both class 3 and 4 didn’t have access to the computers. Jeremy and I decided to have a class competition to entertain the kids. We had them compete and do a variety of things. For example, we had the students arm wrestle, play charades, and spell words in English. At the end, it didn’t really matter who won (even though my team did) because all the students seemed to enjoy the competitive atmosphere. Jeremy and I might work out another class competition next week to review everything we have learned. 

 

      We finally got to playing the recorder today in music class. Thankfully, the kids weren’t too rowdy with their recorders and managed to stop playing after I told them to stop playing. They got the hang of it really quickly actually which surprised me. A majority of them could play all the notes except for the difficult ones like middle C. I think we should probably teach them how to read music on a staff so we can actually play some songs. But, if that doesn’t work, I can always write out the note names for them. We told them to practice tonight so we’ll see how good they are tomorrow. After playing around with the recorder, Kevin taught them about the different beats, complete with the quarter notes, eighth notes, and sixteenth notes. I think the students really enjoyed today’s lesson.

 

      For history, we tried to entice them and keep them interested by saying we would give them the lesson, then go outside and play. We didn’t really get through the lesson but we can always finish it tomorrow. It’d be a good trivia or jeopardy game. So we may review all of it quickly tomorrow, then play a trivia game and establish a winner. I remember back at school, I always loved playing games because I just love competing with other students. I’m sure it’s the same here too. After the lesson, we played a game similar to sharks and minnows with Class 4. I think that we should collaborate more with Class 4 and maybe do some big group activities to make the classes more fun and exciting for the students.

8/13/2010

      Today was the last and final day we have together with the children. The pressure is on for us volunteers to give them a good show! Practice went fine today, and I think Class 3 did great at the performance. I really enjoyed all the effort each and every class put into the performance. It really shows how much these kids have grown. I think the kids especially liked the college students’ performance because it was quite funny, and they were able to sing Chinese songs. The only downside to the performance was the extreme heat. It felt like a freaking sauna outside, and that might have dampened the students’ feelings. Finally, the video of the past 2 weeks was really nice except the screen was too small for anyone to see anything! I think the students had fun dancing on stage though.

 

To tell the truth, I had a bad feeling about today because I really don’t want to leave our YBB family and our students. In these past 2 weeks, we have connected so well with everyone, not just with the volunteers, but with the students. Even before the performance was over, students from our class were crying. It was so hard resisting the urge to join them. When we went back up to the classroom, all I could hear was the sound of sobs and crying that brought tears to my eyes too. I didn’t know what to say or do. I ended up telling them what I felt about the past 2 weeks. I really have enjoyed the past 2 weeks and have had fun despite the living conditions and the work. Even though the preparation for each class is exhausting and I wake up every morning wanting to go back to sleep, teaching class is fun. I knew it was going to be difficult leaving, but I never knew it was going to be this difficult. Our class gave us presents before we left. I got a bamboo heart, 2 bracelets, a ring, and a Winnie the Pooh keychain. By giving these gifts, the students really are showing how much they appreciated us coming. Every time I look at my presents I think back to all those times we had together. Even if some of the students never really listened in class and goofed off, it was all fun. Oh, also, I saw the 2 girls that got kicked out of our class today. They came for the final performance!

 

Now, back to the classroom. Almost every girl in our class was crying when we first went up. The classroom teacher told us that we should get them on their way home quickly because the weather was turning bad. I bet once I check my email when I have internet, students will have already sent me emails. I told them whenever they missed us they could contact us anytime. After my failed attempt at uplifting the mood, we got everyone downstairs to take a group picture. I really wish we could’ve taken the picture before this, I even mentioned it to our college kids but I guess it got jumbled in the mix with the performance. After our group picture, we tried to send everyone home because the weather really was becoming worse. But none of the students budged. The girls were crying, and the guys were waiting. We told them that if they wanted to go home they could and those that wanted to stay could stay for a little while. They all stayed. That really touched me. To think that we improved their lives so much that they refused to go home. If this were in America, the students would have probably bolted at the first glimpse of freedom. But things are different here. These kids really appreciate what we have done. But it’s not just us; these kids have benefited the volunteers as well. Since nobody wanted to go home, we decided to go back to the class instead of standing in the rain. This return to the classroom got everyone. When we first were in the classroom, only the girls cried and the guys pretended to cry. But this time, everyone was crying except for 2 guys. I never knew how much of an impact we had on our kids. We sang some songs together to try and cheer everyone up, but that just brought about more tears and sobs. We asked the students what they wanted to do. Their last and final request. I’ll never forget what they chose. They wanted us to teach them one last lesson. It was so hard resisting the urge to cry. Even our strong boys that pretended to cry were sobbing. All we could do was comfort them and tell them to write to us! If I have the chance, I will come back and visit each and everyone one of them. When we asked them to leave, they all refused. One of the girls that got kicked out sat on the ground and refused to budge. This was one of the most touching moments in my life.

 

I tried to leave on an inspirational note. I told them that they should work and study hard. And one day when they become successful, they can come find us in America. We even taught them one time in class. Impossible is nothing, as long as you put your heart into it.
