Biographies

Jesse Wong, age 18, is a senior graduating from Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology in Alexandria, Virginia. He began piano lessons at the age of 5, and has studied with Nancy O'Neill Breth since 1996. Jesse's first-place awards include the 22nd Bartok-Kabalevsky-Prokofiev International Piano Competition, the International Young Artists Piano Competition, the Friday Morning Music Club's Henbest Competition, the Beethoven Society of America's Maria Fisher Competition, the 3rd Annual Lee University International Piano Competition, and numerous state and local competitions. In October 2006 he won the National Symphony Orchestra's Young Soloists' Concerto Competition, leading to his Kennedy Center orchestral soloist debut with the NSO later that spring. This year, Jesse and George Fu placed first in the 2008 MTNA-Kawai National Senior Piano Duet Competition, held in Denver, Colorado.  Jesse's summer studies, often on scholarship, have included the International Institute for Young Musicians, the Eastern Music Festival, and the Bowdoin International Music Festival.  Jesse will attend Harvard University this fall.

Yvonne Chen, age 18, is a senior at Westfield High School in Chantilly, VA. Currently studying with Ernest Barretta, she travels to New York weekly for The Juilliard School's Pre-College Division where she is involved with the piano performance and chamber music program.
Yvonne has been a winner or finalist of many national and international competitions in the past few years. She was honored as one of the seven national finalists in the 2004 and 2005 MTNA-Baldwin Junior High Piano national competition. She placed third at the MTNA-Baldwin Junior High Piano national competition in April 2005 in Seattle, WA. She was also the winner in the 19th International Young Artist Piano Competition in June 2004, Lee University Piano Competition in January 2005, and Furman University Daniel Piano Competition in March 2007.
A guest soloist for seven years with local orchestras, she was also a guest recitalist and a masterclass participant at the World Piano Pedagogy Conference in Nashville, TN in 2003 and four times invited as a guest soloist for the Alden Theatre Young Musicians Series. She has also been twice invited to give recitals at the U.S. State Deparment's Dean Acheson Auditorium. In 2005 she performed Rachmaninoff's Second Piano Concerto with orchestra in a fund-raising concert for victims of Hurricane Katrina at Reston Pavilion. 
In her spare time, Yvonne teaches piano in Oak Hill, VA and accompanies her high school's choirs as well as HaiTien Chorus. She will be furthering her musical studies in the fall at The Juilliard School in New York under the tutelage of Matti Raekallio.

George Xiaoyuan Fu, 17, is a junior at Urbana High School in Frederick, Maryland. George currently studies piano with Mrs. Susan Starr and Dr. Scott Beard of Shepherd University. His previous teachers include Mrs. Carole Kriewaldt and Mrs. Faye Bonner.
George has been a prizewinner in numerous piano competitions, recently including First Place in the 2008 National Symphony Orchestra Concerto Competition, Washington, DC; First Place in the 2008 MTNA National Senior High Piano Duet Competition, Denver, CO.; Third Place and Audience Favorite Prize in the 2007 International Institute for Young Musicians International Piano Competition, Lawrence, KS; First Place in the 2007 International Young Artists Piano Competition, Washington, D.C.; First Place in the 2006 National MTNA Junior Piano Competition, Austin, TX; First Place in the 2006 Maria Fischer Beethoven Society Piano Competition, Washington, D.C; and many more.  George’s many achievements at the highest level have generated great interest in his fine performances from various venues. He has been invited to perform at the U.S. State Department, the German Embassy in D.C., and Kansas Public Radio at the University of Kansas. On May 21, 2008, he made his successful Solo Debut with the National Symphony Orchestra at the Kennedy Center. And in April 2008, he was selected as a recipient of the National Chopin Foundation Scholarship. 
In addition to piano performances, George is also a self-taught composer, aided with theory and music history studies by Mrs. Carolyn Lamb Booth. He won First Place in the 2006 and 2007 MTNA Maryland State Senior Composition Competition, Second Place in the 2007 MTNA Eastern Division Senior Composition Competition, and Second Place in the 2006 Neva Greenwood Student Composition Competition.
Aside from music, George is an avid sports fan and well versed in art and writing. He is the staff cartoonist and a freelance reporter of his school newspaper, The Hawkeye. He has an interest in languages, being fluent in English, Chinese, and Spanish, and proficient in French. George has also organized, since sixth grade, a bi-monthly concert series at a local retirement home.
